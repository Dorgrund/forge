# Influencer and Promo-Code Terms

`marketing` · `influencers` · `glossary` · **Level:** Beginner

> [!NOTE]
> Plain-English definitions of influencer marketing terms — creator tiers, barter, revenue share, promo codes, engagement — so the acquisition strategy makes sense.

---

## What it is

When you pay or partner with someone who has an audience to promote your app, that person is an **influencer** (also called a **creator**). These terms describe the kinds of influencers and the deals you make with them.

_The core terms, everyday explanation first:_

| Term | Think of it as... | Precise meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nano-influencer** | A friend with a popular local Instagram | A creator with roughly **1,000–10,000** followers. Small but very trusted audience. |
| **Micro-influencer** | A niche YouTuber everyone in a hobby knows | Roughly **10,000–100,000** followers. Bigger reach, still personal. |
| **Macro-influencer** | A minor celebrity | **100,000+** followers. Wide reach, but lower trust and conversion. |
| **Barter deal** | "Promote my app and keep free premium" | You pay with **product instead of cash** — e.g. free lifetime access. |
| **Revenue share** | A salesperson's commission | You pay the creator **a % of sales** they drive (commonly 10–20%). |
| **Promo code** | A discount code at checkout | A code the creator gives their audience for a **discount**, which also **tracks** who came from them. |
| **Engagement rate** | How many fans actually react, not just follow | Share of an audience that **likes/comments/clicks**, not just the follower count. |

---

## Why / when to use it

Smaller creators convert better, which is counter-intuitive but well documented:

_Why nano/micro beats macro for a small budget:_

| Tier | Engagement rate | Conversion vs macro | Typical deal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nano** | often **8%+** | **2–3× higher** | Barter |
| **Micro** | ~**4–7%** | ~**4.1%** (vs 2.6% macro) | Barter or low cash |
| **Macro** | ~**1–2%** | ~**2.6%** | Cash, expensive |

The logic: a nano creator's followers treat a recommendation like advice from a friend, so a higher share of them act. That is why your plan — barter with many small creators — is the right shape, not one big paid celebrity.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Pair every barter deal with a **personal promo code**. **55%** of consumers seek out influencer content more when a discount code is attached, and the code is how you measure which creator actually drove subscribers.

---

## Example

How one nano creator turns into subscribers:

```text
1 nano creator, ~5,000 followers
  x ~8% engaged           = ~400 people see it meaningfully
  x ~3-5% act on the code = ~10-25 subscribers from one strong post
```

So reaching, say, 500 subscribers is really a question of **recruiting ~60–100 creators** (allowing for ones who underperform or never post), not persuading 500 individuals one by one.

---

## Key points / gotchas

- **Smaller can convert better.** Nano/micro audiences trust the creator more, so a higher share buys.
- **Barter + revenue share beats barter alone.** Free premium is a one-time thank-you; a sales cut gives an ongoing reason to keep promoting.
- **The promo code does double duty** — discount for the buyer, and attribution (proof of who drove the sale) for you.
- **Follower count is vanity; engagement is the real signal.** 10,000 engaged followers beat 100,000 silent ones.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Chasing big follower counts.** Macro influencers cost more and convert worse for niche apps.
- **Barter with no tracking.** Without a personal promo code you cannot tell which creator worked.
- **Pure barter, no commission.** One-off free-premium deals fade; creators stop posting.
- **Judging a creator by followers alone** instead of engagement rate.
