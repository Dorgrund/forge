# Creator Outreach and Barter Operations

`marketing` · `influencers` · `outreach` · `operations` · **Level:** Intermediate

> [!NOTE]
> The practical playbook for finding bloggers, how many to contact per day, what to write them, whether a contract is needed, and how to set up the outreach account safely.

---

## What it is

The operational layer beneath the [influencer strategy](subscription-bundle-and-influencer-attribution.md): the day-to-day work of turning "recruit ~60–100 creators" into an actual routine. It answers five questions — where to find creators, how many to message, what to say, what agreement to use, and how to run the outreach account without getting blocked. Terms used here (cold DM, response rate, barter, revenue share) are defined in the [glossary](terms/README.md).

---

## Why / when to use it

Because the bottleneck is not effort — it is **response rate**. Most messages are ignored, so the volume has to be planned backwards from the goal.

_How outreach volume is calculated backwards from the subscriber goal:_

| Step | Number | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Subscriber goal | **~500** | strategy doc |
| Subscribers per active creator | **~10–25** | nano post math |
| Creators needed | **~60–100** | goal ÷ per-creator |
| Creators who say yes after replying | ~**half** of responders | planning assumption |
| Response rate (personalized) | **5–15%** DM, **15–30%** email | outreach benchmarks |
| **Total creators to contact** | **~800–1,000** | working backwards |

At a sustainable **20–30 personalized messages per day**, ~1,000 contacts is **~4–6 weeks** of outreach. Volume is real, but the limiter is personalization and platform limits, not hours.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Email gets roughly 2× the response of cold DMs** and signals you are serious. Use email for volume; reserve DMs for the creators you most want.

## Example

The five operational pieces, each concrete.

_Where to find creators:_

| Method | How | Best for |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Manual search** | Niche + city hashtags on Instagram/TikTok; Reddit subreddits; Facebook groups | Aligned, high-trust nano creators |
| **Discovery tools** | Modash, Heepsy, Collabstr, Ainfluencer — filter by tier, niche, location, engagement | Volume and filtering |

_What to write (reusable template — personalize the bracketed parts):_

```text
Subject: Free lifetime premium + commission — [App] x [their handle]

Hi [name],

I follow your [specific thing — e.g. budgeting tips], and your
[specific post] really matched what I'm building: [App], a [one-line
what it does].

I'd love to partner. The offer:
- Free lifetime premium to [App] (and my other apps).
- Your personal promo code: your followers subscribe at a discount.
- [X]% of every subscription your code brings in.

If you're open to it, I'll send a 1-page summary. No pressure either way.

Thanks,
[Your name] — [business handle / site]
```

_How many per day:_

```text
20-30 personalized messages/day
  -> ~1,000 contacts over ~4-6 weeks
  -> ~15% reply, ~half say yes
  -> ~60-100 creators onboarded
```

_Do you need a contract? Yes — a simple written agreement (full example, signing methods, and the cost of going without are in [creator-agreement.md](creator-agreement.md)). It must cover:_

| Must include | Why |
| :--- | :--- |
| Both parties' real names + contact | Basic enforceability |
| What they post, and when | Defines the deal |
| What they get (premium, code, **%**) | Their side of the barter |
| **FTC disclosure clause** (`#ad`/`#sponsored`) | Legally required — even barter is a "material connection" |
| Indemnification | Shifts disclosure-failure risk to the creator |
| Content review/approval right | Protects your brand |

> [!WARNING]
>
> - FTC disclosure is **mandatory for barter** — free product counts as a material connection, so posts must be marked `#ad`/`#sponsored`.
> - As of 2025 the **brand shares liability**; penalties run up to **$53,088 per violation**. Never tell a creator to hide the `#ad` tag — that makes you co-liable.
> - This is **not legal advice.** FTC is US law; other regions (UK ASA, EU) have equivalents. Have a lawyer review your template before use.

_Setting up the outreach (Instagram business) account:_

| Do | Avoid |
| :--- | :--- |
| Make it a **business/verified** account for credibility | A brand-new account blasting DMs |
| **Warm it up** — post real content, ramp slowly | Mass identical DMs (spam flag + shadow-ban) |
| Use **email for volume**, DMs for top targets | Relying only on DMs from a fresh account |

## Key points / gotchas

- **Plan volume backwards from the goal** — ~1,000 contacts for ~80 creators, because most messages are ignored.
- **Personalization beats volume.** 25 tailored messages outperform 200 copy-pastes and avoid spam flags.
- **Email > DM** for response rate and seriousness; DMs are limited on new accounts.
- **A simple written agreement is required**, mainly for the FTC disclosure clause and indemnification — not a heavyweight contract.
- **Warm up the Instagram account** before outreach, or it gets rate-limited.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Mass-DMing from a brand-new account** — gets shadow-banned, kills the channel before it starts.
- **Skipping the disclosure clause** — exposes you to FTC liability up to $53,088 per violation.
- **Telling creators to hide `#ad`** — makes the brand co-liable, the opposite of protection.
- **Under-counting outreach** — expecting 80 creators from 80 messages; the real number is ~1,000 contacts.
- **Vague pitches** — no specific reference to their content reads as spam and gets ignored.
