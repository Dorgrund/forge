# Disclosure Rules by Country

`marketing` · `legal` · `compliance` · `global` · **Level:** Intermediate

> [!NOTE]
> How influencer ad-disclosure rules differ across the major markets for a globally available app, and the single approach that keeps you compliant in nearly all of them at once.

---

## What it is

When you sell an app globally, your barter creators post in many countries, each with its own ad-disclosure regulator. This doc maps the rules for the biggest markets. The reassuring part: the **core rule is the same everywhere** — a paid or barter relationship (a "material connection") must be **clearly and prominently disclosed** as advertising. What differs is the accepted label, the enforcer, and the penalty.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This is **not legal advice** — it is an orientation map. For markets that matter most to you, confirm the current rule with a local source or lawyer.

---

## Why / when to use it

Because you do not need 50 different posts. You need **one disclosure standard strict enough to satisfy the toughest common rules**, applied everywhere. That standard:

- A clear label — **`#ad`**, `Paid partnership`, or `Advertisement` — in plain language.
- Placed at the **start** of the caption or **overlaid on the video**, not buried in hashtags, comments, or "see more".
- Applied to **all** incentivised content — paid, gifted, free premium, affiliate, or promo-code deals.

Meet that, and you satisfy the FTC, ASA, EU, Canada, and Australia simultaneously. The per-country table below is for when you need the specifics of a particular market.

## Example

The major markets, who enforces, and what is distinctive about each.

_Ad-disclosure rules across the largest markets:_

| Market | Regulator / body | Type | What is distinctive | Action needed? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **United States** | FTC | Government law | "Material connection" must be disclosed; brand shares liability; max civil penalty ~**$53,088/violation** (repeat/willful) | Clear `#ad`, indemnify creator |
| **United Kingdom** | ASA (+ CMA) | Self-reg + law | Label must be **immediate and prominent**; gifts/affiliate/codes all count; CMA now has real fining power | `#ad` at the **start**, not in hashtags |
| **EU (all members)** | DSA + national law | Government law | Platforms must let users flag commercial content (DSA Art. 26); unfair-practices directive applies | Same clear label; honest claims |
| **Germany** | UWG / courts | Government law | **Presumption** the post is paid unless proven otherwise; business creators need an imprint | Disclose by default; do not argue "unpaid" |
| **France** | Influence Act + DGCCRF | Government law | Bans influencer promotion of some categories (crypto, medical, cosmetic surgery); strict sponsored-intent labelling | Avoid banned categories; clear label |
| **Canada** | Ad Standards | Self-reg | Covers **any** exchange of value incl. free product; creator must have **actually used** the product | Clear in-content label; honest use |
| **Australia** | AANA (+ TGA, ACCC) | Self-reg + law | Strict on health/wellness/skincare claims; some fines up to **A$1.3M** | Clear label; no unproven claims |
| **India** | ASCI / IAMAI | Self-reg | Mandatory disclosure label; 2024 rules address AI/deepfake content | Clear label; flag AI content |

## Key points / gotchas

- **The core rule is universal:** disclose any material connection (including free premium) clearly and up front. Differences are in wording and penalty, not principle.
- **Comply with the strictest common standard once** — `#ad` at the start of the post — and you cover nearly every major market.
- **Self-regulatory ≠ harmless.** Bodies like the ASA, Ad Standards (Canada), and ASCI (India) issue public rulings; the UK CMA and US FTC can additionally fine through law.
- **Germany flips the burden of proof** — a post is presumed paid unless the creator shows otherwise, so always disclose.
- **France bans whole categories** (crypto, medical, cosmetic). If your apps ever touch those, check France specifically.
- **Most rules target the creator and platform**, but several (notably the US) make the **brand co-liable** — which is exactly why the disclosure clause lives in your [creator agreement](creator-agreement.md).

---

## Common mistakes

- **Writing one set of rules per country** and drowning in detail — the shared standard covers most cases.
- **Burying the label** in hashtags, comments, or behind "see more" — rejected by the ASA, FTC, and Canada alike.
- **Assuming "unpaid gift" needs no disclosure** — free premium is a material connection everywhere; Germany even presumes payment.
- **Ignoring category bans** (e.g. France on crypto/medical) when an app or claim falls into them.
- **Treating self-regulatory bodies as toothless** — public rulings still cause reputational and platform fallout.
